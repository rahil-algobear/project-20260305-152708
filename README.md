# Deep Ocean Bioluminescence — Shader Art Gallery

An ethereal and dreamy collection of procedural WebGL shader art exploring the mesmerizing phenomenon of bioluminescence in the deep ocean. Each piece is a meditation on the intersection of organic motion and digital luminescence.

## Gallery Pieces

### 1. Abyssal Drift
**Technique:** Flow Field + FBM  
**Palette:** Cyan (#00d4ff) and Violet (#7b2fff)  
**Description:** Jellyfish-like tendrils flow through layered noise currents, creating organic trails of bioluminescent light. The flow field uses curl noise for divergence-free motion, with multiple octaves of FBM creating depth and complexity.

**Interactive:** Move your mouse to influence the current direction and intensity.

### 2. Plankton Constellation
**Technique:** Voronoi + Metaballs  
**Palette:** Mint (#00ffa3) and Cyan (#00d4ff)  
**Description:** Thousands of glowing microorganisms cluster and disperse, their collective light forming shifting constellations. Voronoi cells create organic territories while metaballs simulate the soft glow of bioluminescent plankton.

**Interactive:** Mouse attracts nearby organisms, causing them to cluster around your cursor.

### 3. Bioluminescent Bloom
**Technique:** Reaction-Diffusion  
**Palette:** Magenta (#ff006e) and Violet (#7b2fff)  
**Description:** Coral-like patterns pulse and grow, simulating the chemical reactions that produce living light in deep-sea organisms. The reaction-diffusion system creates organic, self-organizing patterns reminiscent of coral growth.

**Interactive:** Mouse seeds new growth patterns, creating ripples of bioluminescent activity.

### 4. Hadal Vortex
**Technique:** Domain Warping + Kaleidoscope  
**Palette:** Cyan (#00d4ff) and Mint (#00ffa3)  
**Description:** Recursive noise distortions create a hypnotic spiral of bioluminescent energy, drawing the eye into infinite depths. Three layers of domain warping combined with kaleidoscopic symmetry produce mesmerizing fractal-like patterns.

**Interactive:** Mouse controls the number of kaleidoscope segments and spiral intensity.

### 5. Midnight Aurora
**Technique:** Aurora Curtain + FBM  
**Palette:** Violet (#7b2fff) and Magenta (#ff006e)  
**Description:** Vertical waves of bioluminescent light ripple through the water column like an underwater aurora borealis. Ridged FBM creates the characteristic curtain-like appearance of auroras, with horizontal flow simulating water currents.

**Interactive:** Mouse creates ripples in the light curtains, disturbing the flow.

## Technical Details

- **Framework:** Pure WebGL (no external libraries)
- **Shader Language:** GLSL (WebGL2 with WebGL1 fallback)
- **Resolution:** Fully responsive, adapts to any screen size
- **Performance:** Optimized for 60fps on modern hardware
- **Compatibility:** Works in all modern browsers with WebGL support

## How to Run

1. Clone this repository
2. Open `index.html` in a modern web browser
3. No build process or server required!

Alternatively, click on any piece from the gallery homepage to view it fullscreen.

## Performance Notes

All shaders are optimized for real-time rendering:

- Efficient noise functions using hash-based pseudo-random generation
- Limited loop iterations (typically 5-8 octaves for FBM)
- No texture lookups (fully procedural)
- Adaptive quality based on device capabilities
